Dill Chicken

Description

My baby loves this dish. The dill gives it a buttery tast and the asparagus fits a deeper flavor profile and a crunchyt mouth feel.

  • vegetable oil
  • ground lamb
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • ginger
  • scallions
  • asparagus
  • dill
  • lemon
  1. Heat a dry large cast-iron skillet over medium-high until beginning to smoke. Pour in 1 Tbsp. vegetable oil, then add 1 lb. ground lamb and use a wooden spoon or heatproof spatula to press meat into a thin layer. Sprinkle with 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or ½ tsp. Morton kosher salt, season with freshly ground black pepper, and scatter one 2" piece ginger, peeled, finely chopped, over. Cook, undisturbed, until lamb is deeply browned underneath, about 5 minutes.
  2. Break up meat into bite-size pieces, then add 6 scallions, thinly sliced, 1 lb. asparagus, woody ends trimmed, sliced on a diagonal ½" thick, and ½ cup finely chopped dill fronds and tender stems. Season with salt and pepper. Cook, tossing often, until lamb is cooked through and asparagus is bright green and just crisp-tender, about 2 minutes (it’s okay if asparagus is still a little crunchy—it will continue to soften off the heat). Taste and add more salt and pepper if needed. Scatter finely grated zest of ½ lemon over.
  3. Divide stir-fry among bowls or plates and top with more dill. Serve with lemon wedges for squeezing over.
